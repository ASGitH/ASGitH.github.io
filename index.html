Coding if I had to summarize in my own words, is overcoming an issue through creative solutions. Everyone has their own method and there were times where I could recount thinking of solutions way over my head. It's the way I love to work, especially working along with others. Trusting to challenge myself and give it my all to see the project grow. Recollecting my successes and failures, they guided me to who I am, today. My first introduction to game development started around 4 years ago at a skills center. It wasn't until 2 years ago, where my focus started to shift into programming. At that point, I enrolled in the Academy of Interactive Entertainment. To say that I had to work hard to become where I am today as a game programmer is a total understatement, I had to learn from the ground up. But despite what I had to go through, it has have allowed me to explore and experiment. An example of that is learning and developing GameBoy games! Growing up with the GameBoy, that love was able to turn into a passion that blossomed which has stuck, and have been loving it ever since. If there was something I would love for you to take away from this, is who I am truly. To whomever I work with, whether a project or two, or within a larger group, I'm forever thankful for not just the opportunities I'm given, but the relationships I get to form. To learn from one another and also getting share what I have with others. There's never a day that passes and I think about and understand what's best for me, and stepping out of the comfort zone. If I ended up embarrassing myself, just being able to try something new is something the younger version of myself never would have done. Because of that, it has allowed me to go through and volunteer for the community. Meeting amazing people and volunteering at events such as the Seattle Indies Expo, to even volunteering for my school during Pax West. I'm always looking for any opportunities. Whether that would be a job in the video game industry to happily meeting and connecting with new people. Feel free to reach me and I would gladly answer. Thank you, I am very happy and excited to hear from ya!
